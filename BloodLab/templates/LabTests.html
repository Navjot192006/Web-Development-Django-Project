Frequently Asked Questions

How should I prepare for my blood test?

+

Most blood tests require fasting for 8-12 hours beforehand. This means no food or drink except water. Avoid alcohol for 24 hours before your test. Continue taking prescribed medications unless your doctor instructs otherwise. Wear loose clothing that allows easy access to your arm. Stay hydrated by drinking water before your test.

How long does it take to get results?

+

Turnaround times vary by test: Basic tests (CBC, metabolic panel) typically take 24-48 hours. Specialized tests (hormone levels, genetic tests) may take 3-7 days. STAT tests ordered by physicians are prioritized and may be available within hours. You'll receive an email notification when your results are ready in our secure patient portal.

Do I need a doctor's order for blood tests?

+

In most states, you can order your own lab tests without a doctor's order through our direct access testing service. However, some specialized tests may require a physician's order. We recommend consulting with your healthcare provider to determine which tests are appropriate for you. If you don't have a doctor, we can connect you with one of our telemedicine providers.

Will my insurance cover the cost of tests?

+

We accept most major insurance plans. Coverage depends on your specific plan and whether the tests are deemed medically necessary. For self-ordered tests without insurance, we offer affordable cash prices. Our staff can help verify your benefits before your visit. We also offer payment plans for larger test panels.

What safety measures are in place during COVID-19?

+

We've implemented extensive safety protocols: Mandatory masks for all staff and patients, enhanced cleaning between patients, social distancing in waiting areas, HEPA air filtration systems, staff health screenings, and optional curbside check-in. We also offer in-home phlebotomy services for high-risk patients.
