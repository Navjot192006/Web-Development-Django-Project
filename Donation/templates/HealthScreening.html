Your Health Matters

Free Health Check With Every Donation

At LifeBlood, we believe that donating blood should benefit both the recipient and the donor. That's why we provide comprehensive health screening with every donation, giving you valuable insights into your health status at no additional cost.

Our health screening includes:

  • Complete blood pressure measurement (systolic and diastolic)
  • Hemoglobin level testing with immediate results
  • Pulse rate and rhythm assessment
  • Body temperature evaluation
  • Basic cardiovascular health screening
  • Blood type verification (if unknown)
  • Infectious disease testing (post-donation)

These quick and painless tests can help identify potential health issues early, when they're most treatable. Many of our donors have discovered previously unknown health conditions through our screening process.

Understanding Your Results

After your donation, you'll receive a detailed report of your health screening results with explanations of what each measurement means. Our staff is always available to answer any questions you might have about your results.

Blood Pressure: We measure both systolic and diastolic pressure. Normal range is typically 120/80 mmHg or lower. High readings may indicate hypertension, while low readings could suggest hypotension.

Hemoglobin Levels: This measures the oxygen-carrying protein in your blood. For men, normal is 13.5-17.5 g/dL; for women, 12.0-15.5 g/dL. Low levels may suggest anemia, while high levels could indicate other conditions.

Pulse Rate: A normal resting heart rate is between 60-100 beats per minute. We also check for irregular rhythms that might need medical attention.

Temperature: Normal body temperature ranges from 97.8°F to 99.0°F. Elevated temperature may indicate infection or illness.

Remember that our screening is not a substitute for regular medical checkups, but it can serve as an important health indicator between visits to your doctor.

Our Screening Process

Every blood donation includes our comprehensive health screening. Here's what to expect during your appointment:

1

Registration

Complete basic paperwork and provide identification. Our staff will review your health history questionnaire.

2

Health Screening

We'll check your blood pressure, pulse, temperature, and hemoglobin levels. This takes about 10 minutes.

3

Medical Review

A healthcare professional will review your results and discuss any concerns with you privately.

4

Blood Donation

If all screening results are acceptable, you'll proceed to donate blood (about 10 minutes).

5

Results & Refreshments

Receive your health screening report and enjoy some refreshments while you recover.
